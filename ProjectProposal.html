I-Project Proposal

1-Problem understanding and context.

Actually on the news, we hear three subjects more than others.

Subject 1: “Organization of the next election.”

Subject 2: “Organization of the referendum.”

Subject 3: “Insecurities”

The government, principally our actual president needs to organize in less than one year an election and a referendum who will permit him to change the constitution because for him our actual political instability derived from the imperfections of our actual constitution. Immediately after the announcements an attitude of mistrust towards this project has developed in the different layers of the population. Several groups say publicly they are against this project or manifest some restraints about that. We all know how the present situation is with the feeling that you are insecure, the difficulties to go to work. The difficulties to plan and achieve our personal duties.

In regard to all these things, AyitiAnalytics think more than half of Haitian students are against the referendum and AyitiAnalytics need to know why they have this position.

To bring an answer, we will in the first step ask this question: “WHAT DO YOU THINK ABOUT THE REFERENDUM?”  in the second step: “WHY DO YOU HAVE THIS POSITION?” in the last step, we will be able to bring a solution to this question: “WHAT WE CAN DO IF WE WANT TO CHANGE THEIR OPINIONS?”

Knowing what the population thinks is important if we want to know what we can do to solve the problem and mobilize them to support the referendum.

An alternative solution can be to do a media campaign to encourage students to participate in the referendum. But if we want to know the optimal solution, we need to ask them the question and their answer will facilitate us to find this optimal solution.

 

2-Conception project model

The referendum, if he is realized, will have a big impact on the entire society. It doesn't matter if you agree with him or not. Everyone will be impacted: “Students, Civil Society, Informal Workers, Technician, Government structure, your future children etc…”

If we hear news on TV or radio, we can feel a sensation of fear among the people because they think the country will be paralyzed in the next few days and they start to prepare themselves for the next lock season.

To be true, most of them do not really realize the real impact this referendum project will have on their life or they are knowing this but act as if nothing is really happening and they continue to live their life like before.

3-SWOT Analyze.

In this Project, we have doing an SWOT analyze and we have identified these things:

Strength

As a strong point we can use technology to facilitate access to students and thus to know what they are thinking.We can also guarantee the anonymity of each one since no data allowing us to identify the respondents will be collected.

Weaknesses

By using social networks and the internet, we will de facto exclude all students who will not have access to these networks during the survey period. This could make the responses collected less relevant.

Opportunities

This survey will provide access to more data than just the opinions of students.

Social networks also allow us to have faster access to students and data processing is almost automatic.

Threat

The respondent can manifest a reluctance to answer our questions because they can be frustrated. They can have premonitory ideas, such as “we work for money” and else. They can be afraid to answer our questions because they can think this can put them in danger and if something happens they will not be protected. The population do not trust the Haitian government and the current political atmosphere of the country, our survey can be misinterpreted. They can think that we work for Jovenel.

4-Recommendation and Conclusion

As we can see , the participation rate for the students will be very low,less than 15%. This rate can be explained by a cultural context but also by several other things like : “The fact that more than 80% of the population does not believe the government policy”.

We can not say with certainty that who will vote in the referendum will do this because they feel comfortable with their live.It would seem rather that a certain part of the students who will vote will do so because they want to oppose the referendum.

We should also anticipate that the results of the referendum will in no way be representative of the true will of the people because of the very low participation that we will have.

The real problem with government is trust. In almost 3 years of power, the government has not achieved anything really important from the point of view of the population that could have led them to support it.

In only 3 month, it will be very difficult otherwise impossible for the government to make the population believe that it has a real project and mobilize enough part of the population to vote yes for her project.

The only thing that the government can do is to try to buy these votes but even if they do that we can not be sure that will be a success.

Even millions of dollars of marketing and publicity will not resolve this problem. It will be better if this money is used for public investment.

If we want a greater participation rate in the next election, the government must solve several problems such as insecurities,inflation rate,unemployment rate etc…

Those tasks will take more than one year except maybe the kidnapping Gap.
